ChatGPT Is Recommending Other Naturopaths — Here's Why

When a potential patient types "best naturopath in Vancouver" into ChatGPT, they get a confident, specific answer — and it's probably not your name. Here's how AI search citations work, and what you can do about it.

The search shift nobody warned NDs about

For the past decade, "getting found online" meant Google rankings. You optimized your site, built some links, maybe ran a few Google Ads. Most naturopaths still think that's the whole game.

It isn't anymore.

A growing number of people — especially the health-conscious, research-oriented type of patient who tends to book with NDs — now start their search in ChatGPT, Claude, or Google's AI Overviews. They type a question, get a direct answer, and book the practitioner who got cited. They never see your site. They never click a search result. They just act on what the AI told them.

If the AI doesn't know you exist — or doesn't trust your site enough to cite you — you're invisible to that entire cohort of patients.

"If the AI doesn't know you exist, you're invisible to an entire cohort of patients who will never click a Google result."

How AI decides who to recommend

AI language models don't just scrape the web randomly. They synthesize what they "know" from training data and from real-time search when available (like in ChatGPT's browsing mode). The factors that influence whether you get cited include:

1. Structured data (JSON-LD schema)

Sites that use FAQPage, Service, and Organization schema markup are explicitly communicating to machines what they are, what they do, and who they serve. AI tools can parse this directly — it's not inference, it's fact-reading. If your site has no schema, you're relying on the AI to guess correctly from unstructured HTML.

2. Clear, direct answers to specific questions

AI models are trained on question-answer pairs. Sites that clearly answer questions like "What does a naturopath treat?" or "How much does a naturopathy appointment cost?" are much more likely to be cited than sites that just list services in vague paragraphs.

This is why an FAQ section on your site isn't just good for human visitors — it's one of the most powerful AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) signals you can add.

3. Domain authority and backlinks (still matters)

AI tools that use real-time search (like ChatGPT with browsing) still factor in how credible and established a site appears. Links from naturopathic associations, local health directories, and your provincial ND college still carry weight.

4. Location specificity

If your site doesn't clearly state where you practice — in the page title, the address in structured data, and your copy — AI tools will struggle to match you to location-specific queries. A patient asking "naturopath near me" in Toronto should surface your Toronto-based practice. That only happens if your site unambiguously tells search engines (and AI) where you are.

What you can actually do about it

The good news: the things that make you more visible to AI search also make you more visible to traditional Google search. They're aligned, not competing.

Add FAQ schema to every page

Every page on your site that answers a question should have FAQPage JSON-LD schema. This doesn't require a plugin — it's a small block of JSON that you drop before the closing </body> tag. When AI tools encounter a page with this markup, they can extract exact Q&A pairs for citation.

Write like a patient would ask

Structure your content as answers to real questions: "What does a naturopath do for anxiety?" is better than "Anxiety Treatment Services." Use the question as the heading (H2), then answer it in 2–4 sentences. Direct. Clear. Citable.

Claim your Google Knowledge Panel

Your Google Business Profile feeds directly into AI Overviews. A complete, active profile with photos, reviews, services listed, and accurate hours is one of the clearest signals of a real, legitimate local business. AI models factor this in.

Get cited by authoritative ND sites

Links from the CAND directory, your provincial association, naturopathic conference sites, and ND-focused health publications are exponentially more valuable than random backlinks. These establish that you are who you say you are — an important signal for AI trust.

Build a real "About" page

AI models are trained to prefer sites from real, identifiable people. A sparse "About" page with a stock photo and three sentences is a red flag. A page with your full name, credentials (ND designation), graduation school, specializations, and a professional photo is a trust signal — both for patients and for AI.

The window is short

In 2026, most naturopathic practices still haven't optimized for AI search. The practitioners who move now — while the competition is low — will build the kind of domain authority and structured presence that compounds over time.

In two years, every ND will know they need to optimize for AI. The ones who start now will already be in the citation pool. The ones who wait will be fighting for scraps.

This is the same window that existed for Google SEO in 2008. The practices that optimized then still rank at the top today. The AI search window is open right now.
