Jane App vs a Real Website: Do Naturopaths Need Both?

Jane App is the booking and practice management tool of choice for a huge number of Canadian NDs. But many naturopaths make the mistake of using their Jane clinic page as their website. Here's why that's costing them patients — and what to do instead.

What Jane App is actually for

Jane App is a practice management platform. It handles scheduling, patient charting, billing, intake forms, and telehealth. It's genuinely excellent at these things — and it has a public-facing booking page that lets new patients find available appointments.

That booking page is not a website. It's a booking widget with a clinic URL. The distinction matters enormously.

Why Jane's booking page fails as a website

Jane's public clinic page was designed to handle one job: let a patient who already wants to book with you select a time. It does that job well. But it completely fails at the job a website needs to do: convince a stranger to become a patient in the first place.

Jane pages can't rank in Google search

Jane runs on jane.app's domain. Any SEO value from your content goes to Jane, not to you. When someone searches "naturopath Toronto," they see jane.app in the URL — and even if they click, there's no content on the page designed to build trust or explain why they should choose you over anyone else.

Jane pages look generic

Every Jane page looks like every other Jane page. There's no place for your story, your specialty, your approach, your values, or your face. Patients who need a $150+/session practitioner want to feel like they know and trust the person before they book. Jane pages don't give you the space to create that.

Jane pages have no content strategy

You can't add a blog, condition-specific landing pages, or FAQ sections to a Jane page. All of these are the things that drive organic search traffic and help patients find you before they even know your name.

"Jane handles scheduling. Your website handles convincing. Both jobs need to be done — but they need different tools."

What your website does that Jane can't

Your website is your practice's online home. It needs to:

  • Appear in local Google searches for naturopaths in your city
  • Tell potential patients who you are and why they should trust you
  • Explain what conditions you treat and how you approach them
  • Answer the questions people have before they're ready to book
  • Convert curious visitors into confident patients ready to schedule

None of these things happen on a Jane page. They happen on a real website — one you control, one that reflects your brand, one that ranks in search.

How Jane and your website work together

The setup that works best for most NDs is simple:

  1. Your website attracts patients through search, tells your story, and builds trust
  2. Your website CTA links directly to your Jane booking widget when the patient is ready to schedule
  3. Jane handles the booking, intake forms, reminders, and all the practice management from there

You don't need to choose. You embed your Jane booking link directly on your site — usually as a prominent button ("Book an appointment →") and optionally as an embedded widget on a dedicated Booking page. Jane handles the scheduling mechanics; your website handles everything before that moment.

What kind of website do you need?

This depends on where you are in your practice.

Just starting out or budget is tight: A single, well-built landing page with your services, your photo, your location, and a link to Jane is infinitely better than using Jane as your only online presence. This is what our Launch plan provides — one page, done right.

Established and growing: A 5–7 page site with dedicated service pages, an About page, and a blog will drive meaningful organic traffic and significantly increase conversions. This is the Practice plan.

Building a dominant online presence: If you want to rank for specific conditions and be visible city-wide, a larger site with condition landing pages, local SEO content, and a consistent blog strategy is what moves the needle. This is the Authority plan.

In every case, the booking infrastructure is Jane. The website is what brings the patient to the door.
