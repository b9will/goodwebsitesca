The Google Business Profile + Local SEO Checklist for Naturopathic Doctors

A 10-point local visibility audit. Get the profile right, connect it to a strong website, build verifiable professional trust, and track what drives actual bookings.

Your Google Business Profile is often the first place a new patient finds you — before they visit your website. A complete, accurate, well-maintained profile strengthens local search rankings and gives patients the confidence to book. This checklist walks through the five areas that matter most, in the order they matter.

01

Foundation

Get the business identity right first.

1

Claim, verify, and secure the correct profile

Confirm that the practice is eligible, claim the right listing, complete verification, remove or resolve duplicates, and protect owner access with current account security.

  • The correct profile is claimed and verified
  • Duplicate profiles are resolved
  • Primary owners and managers are current
  • Account recovery and security are documented

Quick test: Could the practice recover control of its profile today?

2

Use an accurate name and primary category

Use the real-world business name without added keywords. Choose the most accurate primary category, then add only secondary categories that reflect services actually offered.

  • Business name matches real-world branding
  • No keywords are added to the name
  • Primary category describes the core business
  • Secondary categories are accurate and limited

Quick test: Would a new patient immediately understand what this business is?

02

Completeness

Complete every useful practice detail.

3

Complete contact, hours, and booking information

Keep the address or service area, phone, website, appointment link, regular hours, special hours, and accessibility details complete and current.

  • Address or service area is accurate
  • Phone and website links work
  • Appointment link opens the right booking flow
  • Regular and special hours stay current

Quick test: Could a patient call, visit, or book without encountering stale information?

4

Describe services and useful profile attributes

Add the services patients actually search for, with clear and compliant descriptions. Complete relevant attributes without stretching categories or making unsupported claims.

  • Core services are listed individually
  • Descriptions use patient-friendly language
  • Relevant attributes are completed
  • Services match what the website offers

Quick test: Does the profile fully explain what someone can book?

03

Proof

Show a real, active practice.

5

Publish authentic photos and videos

Use current images of the exterior, entrance, reception, treatment environment, practitioners, and accessibility features. Avoid stock imagery that could misrepresent the location.

  • Exterior and entrance are recognizable
  • Interior photos reflect the current space
  • Practitioner images are professional and current
  • Accessibility features are visible where relevant

Quick test: Would a first-time visitor recognize the clinic when they arrive?

6

Build verifiable professional trust signals

Professional trust on your GBP profile is built through accurate, complete content — not through review volume. Focus on the signals that are both verifiable and compliant with provincial professional rules.

  • Credentials, designations, and education are listed accurately
  • Professional photos are current and high quality
  • The Q&A section is active with practitioner-written answers
  • Services are described without unverifiable outcome claims

Quick test: Do the trust signals on this profile reflect what a cautious patient would need to feel confident?

ⓘ

Ontario NDs — important compliance note The College of Naturopaths of Ontario prohibits NDs from using patient testimonials in advertising and advises practitioners not to encourage patients to post public reviews. This means Ontario NDs should not actively solicit reviews, create a review request process, or highlight review volume as a trust signal. If a patient leaves a review unsolicited, responding professionally (without confirming a clinical relationship) is appropriate. When in doubt, consult the College's current advertising guidelines.

04

Alignment

Connect the profile to a strong local website.

7

Respond to reviews professionally and privately

Thank reviewers without confirming a clinical relationship or discussing care. Use calm, consistent responses and move sensitive concerns to an appropriate private channel.

  • Responses never reveal patient information
  • Positive and critical reviews receive attention
  • Sensitive issues move to a private channel
  • Tone stays calm, useful, and professional

Quick test: Could any response accidentally confirm someone is a patient?

8

Match the website and profile details

Point the profile to the most useful local page and keep the business name, address, phone, hours, services, booking destination, and practitioner details aligned.

  • Profile links to the best local landing page
  • Name, address, and phone match
  • Hours and services agree
  • LocalBusiness structured data reflects visible information

Quick test: Would Google and a patient see one consistent business entity?

05

Prominence & Measurement

Build local recognition and monitor it.

9

Strengthen legitimate local prominence

Earn accurate listings and relevant mentions from professional associations, local organizations, trusted directories, community partners, and useful editorial coverage.

  • Key professional listings are accurate
  • Local directories use consistent details
  • Relevant organizations link to the site
  • Inauthentic mentions are avoided

Quick test: Do trusted local sources confirm this practice exists and matters?

10

Review profile performance every month

Track searches, views, calls, website visits, bookings, photo freshness, edits, and policy alerts. Assign ownership before problems become urgent.

  • Profile performance is reviewed monthly
  • Booking and website actions are tracked
  • New edits and alerts are checked
  • One person owns ongoing maintenance

Quick test: Can you explain which profile actions lead to appointments?

What your score means

0–3: Foundation missing. Resolve profile ownership, accuracy, and essential trust gaps first — they block everything downstream.

4–6: Credible start. Choose the gaps with the greatest effect on patients and local discoverability.

7–9: Strong profile. Close the remaining gaps and make monthly reviews a standing task.

10/10: Ready to compound. Maintain the standard, measure outcomes, and keep improving.

Guidance used: Google Improve local ranking; Google LocalBusiness structured data. Ontario-specific compliance guidance from the College of Naturopaths of Ontario advertising guidelines.

Your first improvement sprint

Identify the single highest-impact gap from your score above. Name the owner, set a target date, and define how you'll know it's improved. That one decision is worth more than a perfect checklist.
