The AI Citation Ready Article Checklist for Naturopathic Doctors

A 10-point evidence-led publishing audit. Create original, expert-led health articles that answer real questions, support claims credibly, stay indexable, and give AI search systems material worth citing.

ChatGPT, Google's AI Overviews, and Perplexity cite sources directly in their answers. For naturopathic doctors, that means original, expert-led content that passes a basic credibility check is the most valuable thing you can publish. This checklist is about producing articles that a skeptical AI — or a skeptical patient — would feel confident citing. No agency can guarantee inclusion, but strong fundamentals are the only foundation that works.

01

Question

Start with a question worth answering.

1

Choose one real patient question and purpose

Define the audience, the decision or problem, and what a satisfying answer should help them understand. Keep the article aligned with the site's genuine subject expertise.

  • One clear question anchors the article
  • The intended reader is defined
  • The topic fits the site's expertise
  • The article has a useful patient-first purpose

Quick test: Would the intended reader seek this answer even without search engines?

2

Give a concise answer before the deep explanation

State the useful answer early, then add context, evidence, nuance, limitations, and next steps. Avoid forcing readers through a long introduction before addressing the question.

  • A direct answer appears near the beginning
  • Important caveats appear with the answer
  • The opening matches the article title
  • The rest of the article expands usefully

Quick test: Can the core answer be understood from the opening section?

02

Authority

Put a qualified expert behind the answer.

3

Assign a qualified author and reviewer

Name the author, connect the byline to a detailed biography, and add an appropriate clinical reviewer when the topic benefits from additional oversight.

  • The author is named clearly
  • Author expertise matches the topic
  • Reviewer is credited where appropriate
  • Bylines link to substantial biographies

Quick test: Can a reader verify why these people are qualified to answer?

4

Contribute non-commodity expert value

Add original explanations, frameworks, decision points, careful examples, or professional perspective. Do not merely rewrite the current search results in different words.

  • The article contributes original value
  • Expert insight is specific, not decorative
  • Examples are useful and privacy-safe
  • The article goes beyond common summaries

Quick test: What would another page need to cite this article for?

03

Evidence

Make important claims traceable.

5

Use authoritative and primary sources

Prefer current guidelines, systematic reviews, primary studies, government or institutional sources, and recognized professional bodies as appropriate for the claim.

  • Sources are authoritative for the subject
  • Primary sources are used when practical
  • Evidence is current enough for the claim
  • Source quality is considered, not just quantity

Quick test: Would these sources satisfy a careful reader checking the claim?

6

Place citations beside the claims they support

Use descriptive links or reference markers close to the relevant sentence. Avoid dumping an unexplained bibliography at the end or linking to secondary summaries unnecessarily.

  • Citations sit close to supported claims
  • Links use meaningful descriptions
  • Readers can reach the original source
  • Sources are not misrepresented

Quick test: Can every important factual claim be traced without detective work?

04

Structure

Organize for readers, not AEO folklore.

7

Use descriptive headings and natural answer sections

Let headings reflect the real questions and decisions in the topic. Use the length each section needs rather than splitting content into tiny artificial chunks for AI optimization.

  • Headings accurately describe each section
  • Questions reflect real reader needs
  • Sections are complete without filler
  • No artificial chunking is used for AI

Quick test: Does the structure improve understanding for a human reader?

8

Keep important information textual and accessible

Publish essential facts in readable HTML text. Use relevant images, diagrams, tables, captions, and alternative text to support the answer without hiding it inside media.

  • Core answers are available as HTML text
  • Images add meaningful information
  • Alternative text is useful and concise
  • Tables and embeds work on mobile

Quick test: Would the article remain useful if the images failed to load?

05

Eligibility & Learning

Stay indexable, current, and measurable.

9

Confirm search and snippet eligibility

Make sure the page is public, crawlable, indexable, internally linked, canonical, and eligible to show a search snippet. Use only accurate structured data that matches visible content.

  • Googlebot can access a working page
  • No accidental noindex or snippet block exists
  • Canonical and internal links are correct
  • Structured data matches visible content

Quick test: Can Search Console confirm the preferred page is eligible to appear?

10

Review accuracy, AI use, and performance

Disclose material automation use when appropriate, avoid scaled low-value publishing, review factual accuracy, and monitor search visibility, engagement, conversions, and AI reporting.

  • Material automation use is disclosed where useful
  • Human review protects accuracy and originality
  • Updates reflect meaningful editorial work
  • Search and conversion performance are monitored

Quick test: Is this article improving through evidence rather than publishing volume?

What your score means

0–3: Foundation missing. Without a clear question, qualified author, and traceable claims, AI systems have little to work with. Start with checks 1–3.

4–6: Credible start. The article has a foundation but is likely missing original value, citation discipline, or technical eligibility.

7–9: Strong authority content. Close the remaining gaps — usually structure or monitoring — and the article will compound in both search and AI systems.

10/10: Citation-ready. Maintain accuracy, keep sources current, and monitor what's happening in AI search reporting.

Guidance used: Google AI optimization guide; Google People-first content guidelines; Google Generative AI content guidance.

Your first improvement sprint

Pick your most important health article — the one that should be driving qualified traffic — and run it through this checklist. Fixing one article properly is worth more than publishing three new ones that fail check 3.
