The Service Page SEO + AEO Checklist for Naturopathic Doctors

A 10-point patient-first landing page audit. Build focused service pages that answer real questions, demonstrate expertise, support discovery by Google and AI systems, and make booking easy.

Most naturopath websites have one "Services" page listing everything. That page ranks for nothing specific and converts nobody in particular. This checklist is for building individual service pages — one per area of focus — that rank for the searches your patients are actually making and give them everything they need to book.

01

Strategy

Give each page one clear job.

1

Choose one meaningful patient intent

Build the page around one service or closely related need that the practice genuinely addresses. Avoid splitting near-identical keyword variations into thin duplicate pages.

  • One primary patient need anchors the page
  • The service is actually offered
  • Intent differs from other service pages
  • Keyword variants are not turned into duplicates

Quick test: Can the purpose of this page be stated in one sentence?

2

Write a unique title, H1, and search summary

Use a concise title and H1 that accurately describe the service. Include location only when it is useful and truthful, then write a compelling human-readable meta description.

  • Title is unique, concise, and accurate
  • H1 clearly names the page topic
  • Location appears only where useful
  • Meta description supports a useful click decision

Quick test: Would the search result accurately set expectations for the page?

02

Answer

Answer the important questions early.

3

Explain what it is, who it is for, and what happens

Open with a plain-language explanation of the service, the people it may suit, what an appointment involves, and the appropriate next step.

  • The service is defined in plain language
  • The intended patient or need is clear
  • The appointment process is summarized
  • A sensible next step appears early

Quick test: Can a patient understand the offer without decoding clinical jargon?

4

Cover the full decision journey

Answer the questions people need before booking: approach, practitioner, visit format, fees, timing, location, limitations, preparation, and what follows the first appointment.

  • Approach and visit format are explained
  • Practitioner and credentials are visible
  • Fees, timing, and location are clear
  • Limitations and expectations are addressed

Quick test: What important booking question would still force a phone call?

03

Trust

Demonstrate expertise responsibly.

5

Add practitioner expertise and credible support

Connect the service to a real practitioner with relevant credentials, experience, and professional perspective. Cite authoritative sources for important health claims.

  • A qualified practitioner is identified
  • Relevant credentials link to a useful bio
  • Important claims have credible support
  • Original expert value is present

Quick test: Why should someone trust this page over a generic clinic template?

6

Use accurate, compliant outcome language

Avoid guarantees, exaggerated certainty, fear, or universal claims. Explain uncertainty, individual variation, appropriate referral boundaries, and what the service does not promise.

  • No guaranteed outcomes are promised
  • Individual variation is acknowledged
  • Claims follow professional college rules
  • The page avoids diagnosing the reader

Quick test: Is the page persuasive without overstating what care can achieve?

04

Discoverability

Make the page easy to crawl and understand.

7

Use clear headings and accessible textual content

Organize the page around meaningful questions and sections. Keep important information in readable HTML text rather than hiding it in images, tabs, or scripts that fail to render.

  • Headings describe the content below
  • Important answers exist as HTML text
  • Lists and paragraphs are easy to scan
  • Images have useful alternative text

Quick test: Could a reader or crawler outline the page from its headings alone?

8

Connect the page through useful internal links

Link from navigation, related services, practitioner bios, location pages, and relevant articles. Use descriptive anchors and give readers a logical path in both directions.

  • The page is reachable through crawlable links
  • Related pages link to it contextually
  • Anchor text describes the destination
  • The page links onward to useful next content

Quick test: Can a patient reach this page without relying on site search?

05

Conversion & Validation

Make it work, measure it, improve it.

9

Validate mobile experience and relevant markup

Test speed, stability, forms, and readability on mobile. Add only structured data that applies, matches visible content, and follows specific search feature guidelines.

  • Mobile loading and layout are reliable
  • Forms and booking links work
  • Markup matches visible page content
  • Rich Results Test shows no critical errors

Quick test: Does the page remain fast, clear, and truthful on a phone?

10

Use a clear booking action and track it

Offer a direct, specific next step at useful points on the page. Measure booking clicks or completions, search queries, engagement, and changes after meaningful updates.

  • Primary CTA states what happens next
  • Booking opens the correct destination
  • Conversion actions are measured
  • Search and page performance are reviewed

Quick test: Can you tell whether this page creates qualified booking activity?

What your score means

0–3: Foundation missing. Strategy and answer gaps mean the page is unlikely to rank or convert. Start with checks 1–4.

4–6: Credible start. The page is findable but likely missing trust signals, internal links, or a measurable CTA.

7–9: Strong page. Close the remaining gaps — usually mobile validation or conversion tracking — and the page will compound.

10/10: Patient-first and search-ready. Monitor performance and keep the content current.

Guidance used: Google SEO Starter Guide; Google AI optimization guide; Google Structured data guidelines.

Your first improvement sprint

Pick your highest-impact service page — the one that should be driving the most bookings — and run it through this checklist first. One strong page beats ten weak ones.

The 10 checks

  7. Clear headings & text
  8. Internal links
  9. Mobile & markup validation
  10. Booking CTA & tracking
